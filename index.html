About Me

Growing up, complex systems on the cutting edge of computer science always exhilarated me. Curious about the next big thing in tech conferences such as the Consumer Electronics Show, I constantly fed my curiosity by watching instructional videos and reading articles on code applications such as interpolation of low frame footage and AI learning how to play chess. I was introduced to computer science in high school competitive robotics, which brought me pure joy and excitement from seeing the robot move precisely according to my commands.

This passion and hunger for knowledge have brought me to college as a first-generation college student. I grew a passion and a sense of belonging for coding when I took on more projects and saw the impact on colleagues and teammates, making me wonder about the potential impact I could make if I learned more. My responsibility from that point on has become to benefit humanity with the knowledge acquired throughout my educational career and the years to come. With my enthusiasm for computer science and machine learning, this philosophy has drawn me to the potential future with automation and its democratization to the general public.
